# Open Consumables

> **Measured baselines for everyday hardware.**

Open Consumables is a measurement archive for everyday hardware. We don't manufacture, certify, or curate. We document the accidental open-hardware standards already running inside the consumer-goods aftermarket, freeze them as dated measured baselines, and publish so anyone can keep buying, printing, or batch-producing to fit.

## What this site covers

- **[Project overview](project/overview.md)**, what we do, what we don't do, and why
- **[The open consumables concept](project/concept.md)**, the framing, the wedges, the prior art we look up to
- **[Categories](categories/index.md)**, the consumable categories under measurement, starting with electric toothbrush heads

## Current focus

The pilot category is **electric toothbrush heads**, working name **OpenBrush**. Five additional categories are scaffolded but deferred: air filters, water cartridges, robot vacuum consumables, electric razor heads, scooter parts, humidifier wicks.

See [Toothbrushes](categories/toothbrushes/index.md) for the active work.

## Reference models

We look up to USB-C, VESA, MX-compatible, QMK as aspirational reference models. The realistic near-term mechanic is closer to ISO fasteners, generic pharma equivalents, and hospital procurement catalogs: substitutability of supply, not network effects.

## Status

Pre-product. Public documentation reflects measurements as they land, not before. Specs are dated baselines, not stewarded standards.
